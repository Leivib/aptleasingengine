// the rules

Boring on purpose, where it counts.

The rules the engine will not break, in writing.

It never invents

No promised callbacks, no made-up confirmations, no quoted rent you did not approve. When it cannot answer, it hands off to a person with the whole conversation attached.

It texts legally

Consent recorded, STOP and HELP honored on every thread, first message carries an opt-out, and sends stay inside a Monday to Saturday, 9 to 5 window.

Your data is walled off

Each building's data is isolated, and the isolation is verified by automated tests on every change.

Nothing gets lost

Every conversation and every stamp is backed up nightly, off-site. The proof survives anything.

Done with you, not dumped on you.

A real account manager sets the engine up on your facts, then stays in it: a weekly call or a shared WhatsApp group, whichever fits how you work.

// straight answers

No fine print

What does it cost?

A commission per move-in, agreed up front. No per-door fee and no monthly minimum. If we do not fill units, you do not pay.

What channels does it use?

Text first, because that is what renters answer. Email as well, including replies inside the renter's Zillow thread. Voice is on the roadmap.

Which lead sources does it catch?

Zillow, Apartments.com, and whatever ILS you list on. New sources take about a day to add.

What about my data?

Each building's data is isolated, verified by automated tests, and backed up nightly off-site.

How long does onboarding take?

A day or two. One kickoff call with your team and we are live. Your second building goes even faster.

Does it replace my onsite team?

No. It runs the lead queue and the tour calendar so your team can run the building. Anything needing judgment goes to a person, with the whole conversation attached.
